Common Questions

Everything people ask before ordering.

Are you a law firm?

No — and we never pretend to be. We're a title search and document service. We retrieve official records, prepare standardized government applications, and explain everything in plain language. We don't give legal advice, and when a matter genuinely needs a lawyer, we say so for free.

Is your report the same record a lawyer would pull?

Yes. We retrieve the current parcel register from Ontario's official land registration system — the same underlying record a law office orders. The difference is the flat $79 fee and a summary written for humans.

Which title changes can you handle?

Standardized applications: survivorship (removing a deceased joint owner), name changes after marriage, divorce, or legal name change, estate transmissions to an executor, and corrections to how a name appears. Full ownership transfers — selling or gifting property to a new owner — legally require a lawyer in Ontario, and we'll tell you that up front at no charge.

Why do law offices charge $1,000 for a title change?

Hourly billing, office overhead, and the fact that most clients never learn these are standardized government applications with fixed government fees. It's not a scandal, it's just a market that hasn't had a flat-fee alternative. Now it does.

How fast is "24 hours" really?

Most reports go out same-day during business hours. Twenty-four hours from payment is the guarantee, not the average.

Can you search any property in Ontario?

Any property registered in Ontario's land registration system — which is virtually all of the province. In the rare case we can't retrieve a record, you get a full refund.

What do I need to provide for a title change?

It depends on the change: typically the property address, ID, and a supporting document — a death certificate for survivorship, a marriage certificate or name-change certificate for name updates, or estate documents for transmissions. We confirm the exact list during your free eligibility check.

Is my information private?

Yes. Your details are used only to fulfil your order, never sold or shared. Land registry records themselves are public information in Ontario — that's what makes this service possible.

What if my situation is complicated?

Then we tell you before you pay. Some files need a lawyer, and pretending otherwise helps nobody — the eligibility check exists to sort that out honestly.

Do you serve businesses and professionals?

Yes — contractors checking properties before large jobs, realtors, property managers, and investors are regulars. Ask about volume pricing if you order several reports a month.
